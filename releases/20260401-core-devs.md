### (1) Staked HNT Position Transfers

#### Motivation

Staked HNT positions are represented as NFTs held in the staker's wallet. These NFTs are currently frozen, preventing any transfers. Over the course of a staking position — especially ones locked for extended periods — users have legitimate operational and security reasons for needing to transfer their positions to a different wallet:

- A wallet may become compromised, requiring urgent migration of assets.
- Users may want to move positions into a multisig for improved security.
- Staking may have been set up on a hot wallet, and the user wants to migrate to a cold wallet.

Currently, there is no way to accomplish this without waiting for the position to fully unlock and withdrawing.

#### Implementation

A new transfer endpoint will be added to the staking smart contracts. This endpoint will allow the holder of a staked position to temporarily unfreeze the associated NFT, transfer it to a specified destination wallet, and re-freeze it. The position itself (lock duration, staked amount, rewards, etc.) remains unchanged — only the owning wallet changes.

This is implemented as a dedicated transfer function rather than simply unfreezing the NFTs, because users often do not realize their staked positions are represented by NFTs. If the NFTs were simply unfrozen, users could inadvertently burn them, permanently losing access to their staked funds.

#### Alternatives Considered

- **Unfreezing the NFTs directly**: Rejected because users frequently do not understand that staked positions are represented as NFTs. Unfrozen NFTs can be accidentally burned, which would permanently lock the staked funds with no possibility of recovery.

#### Impact and Stakeholder Impact

- All HNT stakers gain the ability to transfer their staked positions between wallets without unstaking.
- Improves security posture for stakers by enabling migration to more secure wallet setups.

#### Drawbacks

- Adds a new endpoint to the staking smart contracts, increasing the attack surface that must be audited and maintained.
